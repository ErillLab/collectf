[expand/collapse] Euclidean distance (ED) is the distance between two points in $n$-dimensional space.

The ED for two DNA motif columns $X$ and $Y$ is defined as

$$ ED(X,Y) = \sqrt{\sum_{b\in B} (f_X(b)-f_Y(b))^2} $$
where $B = (A,C,G,T)$, $f_X(b)$ is the frequency of base $b$ in column $X$.

The ED between two motifs is defined as the sum of the column comparison scores of the ungapped alignment of the two motifs.
