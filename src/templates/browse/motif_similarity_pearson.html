[expand/collapse] Pearson Correlation Coefficient (PCC) gives a measure of correlation between base frequencies of two motif columns.

Given two motif columns $X$ and $Y$, PCC is defined as

$$ PCC(X,Y) = \frac{\sum_{b \in B} (f_X(b) - \bar{f_X}) \cdot (f_Y(b) - \bar{f_Y})} {\sqrt{\sum_{b \in B} (f_X(b) - \bar{f_X})^2 \cdot \sum_{b \in B} (f_Y(b) - \bar{f_Y})^2}} $$
where $B = (A,C,G,T)$, $f_X(b)$ is the frequency of base $b$ on column $X$ and $\bar{f_X}$ is the average frequency of column $X$.

The PCC between two motifs is defined as the sum of the column comparison scores of the ungapped alignment of the two motifs.
